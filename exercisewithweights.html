Exercise with Weights

What is exercising with weights?

Exercising with weights means to incorporate weights like dumbbells, barbells and kettlebells into your exercise regime. You can use free-weights or machine weights. Free-weights include dumbbell and kettlebells while machine weights include equipment like the chest fly machine, lat pull-down machine, and leg press machine.

Why exercise with weights?

The principle of progression says that as your body adapts to your fitness routine you have to challenge yourself to keep seeing progress. Eventually, I found Pamela Reif's workouts manageable and wanted more kick to my exercise routine so that I could grow stronger. Hence, I started exercising with weights.

Exercising with weights helps to build your strength and muscle definition. According to Verywell Fit,

The only way your body physically changes and grows is if the muscles are taxed to the point where they must grow stronger to lift that weight. When the muscle fibers are taxed in this manner, it causes micro-tears in the fibers. When you rest, these repair themselves and grow back stronger than before. The overload process causes the muscle fibers to grow stronger (and sometimes bigger) in order to handle the additional weight.

If you want to grow stronger and look more toned, exercising with weights are the way to go!

What are some exercises I can follow?

You can start off by using household items like 1.5L water bottles, bleaching lotion bottles, or books as free weights at home! But do take precaution when you use these free weights, as they may feel heavy if you are a beginner. Take care and don't injure yourself!
